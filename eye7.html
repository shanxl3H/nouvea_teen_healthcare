What is Eye Strain?

Eye strain, also referred to as asthenopia, occurs when your eyes become fatigued or overworked from activities such as prolonged computer use, reading, or driving. It's often associated with symptoms like eye discomfort, dryness, redness, headaches, and difficulty focusing. Factors contributing to eye strain include improper lighting, glare, poor posture, uncorrected vision problems, and staring at screens for extended periods without breaks.

What cause Eye Strain?

Eye strain can be caused by various factors, including prolonged use of digital screens like computers, smartphones, and tablets, which can lead to focusing fatigue and dry eyes due to reduced blinking. Poor lighting conditions, such as glare or dim lighting, can also contribute to eye strain by forcing the eyes to work harder to see clearly. Additionally, uncorrected vision problems like nearsightedness, farsightedness, or astigmatism can strain the eyes when trying to focus. Other factors such as improper posture, inadequate breaks during visually intense activities, and underlying conditions like dry eye syndrome or eye muscle imbalance can further exacerbate eye strain.

Tips for Eye Strain

1. Follow the 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 minutes, take a 20-second break, and look at something 20 feet away to give your eyes a rest from focusing on nearby objects.

2. Ensure proper lighting: Avoid glare from windows or overhead lights by adjusting blinds or using curtains. Position your computer screen to minimize reflections and glare.

3. Optimize your workspace: Position your computer screen about an arm's length away and slightly below eye level. Adjust the brightness and contrast of your screen to a comfortable level.

4. Take regular breaks: Give your eyes a break from screens by taking short breaks throughout the day. Use this time to stretch, blink, and focus on objects at different distances.

5. Blink frequently: Blinking helps moisten your eyes and prevent dryness, especially when staring at screens for extended periods.

6. Use artificial tears: If your eyes feel dry or irritated, use over-the-counter artificial tears to lubricate your eyes and relieve discomfort.

7. Practice good posture: Maintain proper posture to reduce strain on your neck, shoulders, and eyes. Sit up straight with your feet flat on the floor and your back supported by your chair.

8. Adjust screen settings: Increase font sizes and adjust screen brightness and contrast to reduce eye strain. Consider using blue light filters or night mode settings, especially before bedtime.

9. Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day to keep your body and eyes hydrated. Dehydration can exacerbate eye dryness and discomfort.

10. Get regular eye exams: Schedule regular eye exams with an optometrist or ophthalmologist to monitor your eye health and address any vision problems that may contribute to eye strain.
